Why do we take life for granted?

An intertemporal question, now relevant more than ever.

xx-April-2021

In the past year, I consciously started overthinking and overanalysing life as a concept. I have at all times been questioning that, though last year, this became more intense. And the main reason, I assume, is the drastic changes our lives have undergone and continue to do so. One by making the appropriate questions and correct adjustments on this topic can go a long way. In my opinion, though, the bottom line is to feel confident with yourself, be grateful for what you have in your life, appreciate the people that are part of your journey and likewise be on their side.

However, it is evident and alerting that frequently we are incapable of appreciating and neglecting small everyday humane gestures and interactions. Especially nowadays when the so invaluable in-person contact, be it work, education or even entertainment has been digitalised on a high percentage we ought to cherish the small things even more. Also, people tend to be inhibited around others and with what purpose? If I were to ask you now, "How well do you know yourself?" think for a moment how clear and honest would be the answer in your head? Why act towards people around us with ill-mannered when we can earn so much more with solidarity from each other. Honestly, I can't emphasise enough the importance of self-acceptance and the result this will have on you once achieved! Take the time and effort required to figure out yourself. It will be a fascinating journey that will pay off to the fullest.

Another topic I would like to touch upon is the things we take for granted. Most notably our possessions, health and loved ones. But also the non-essential diversions that overshadow what matters. For example, short term happiness, senseless attention, self-value proof etc. In our modern society, weights seem to have shifted from the former to the latter. We are keen on or even obsessed with buying stuff we don't need to impress people we don't know whilst not valuing appropriately essentials we enjoy that others wish for those. Why do we truly understand the value of something only when we are on the verge of losing it? What would your stance be if tomorrow you be deprived of clean water, food, your roof, education and so on?

But don't get me wrong, my point is not to settle for less but appreciate and be thankful for what you own now and keep striving for anything else you desire. If you are mindful when you give sincere appreciation for something, you will notice a change in your attitude towards the world. When it comes to relationships with your friends and loved ones, don't wait to express what you feel because time waits for nobody. We tend to go through our days taking all these as given until they are not.

Why live tomorrow when you can live now? Life is unpredictable as it can change in the blink of an eye. Do make the most of it now because that's all you're guaranteed. Don't lay around doing nothing because you think you'll have plenty of time to get it all done tomorrow.

In all situations in life, we have to try and be optimistic and look for the bride side. And the pandemic is no different. Respectfully, I can name several positive outcomes both for humanity and the environment that we encounter daily. Reportedly consumerism has been reduced. Humans realised that so much of our consumer behaviour is about instant gratification, not lasting happiness. Another significant adaptation was to slow down and put less pressure on ourselves. We live in a fast-paced, ever-evolving society and trying to keep up is a demanding task that hinders other areas of our lives. We rush through life by constantly pressuring ourselves to achieve goals and losing focus on what matters most in life, the people around us who make it all worth it. As a result, we started prioritising family and friends again, spending more time with each other and strengthening our bonds.
